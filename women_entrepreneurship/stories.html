Priya Sharma
Handicrafts · Jaipur, Rajasthan
"I started with just ₹8,000 and a small room. Today I have 12 women working with me and we export to 5 countries."
Priya was a homemaker who learned embroidery from her mother. After her husband lost his job in 2018, she decided to turn her skill into a business. She registered on ShaktiUdyam, applied for a Mudra loan of ₹2 lakh, and launched Priya Handicrafts. Within two years, her traditional Rajasthani embroidery products were selling on Amazon and Etsy. She now earns over ₹4 lakh per month and has employed 12 women from her neighbourhood.
Mudra Yojana Beneficiary

Meena Patel
Food & Beverages · Ahmedabad, Gujarat
"Nobody believed a woman from a small town could build a food brand. I proved them wrong."
Meena started making traditional Gujarati snacks from her kitchen during the COVID lockdown. Her neighbours loved the taste and encouraged her to sell. She attended a free STEP training program which taught her food packaging and business basics. She registered her brand Meena Foods, got a small loan from Stand-Up India, and now supplies to over 50 retail stores across Gujarat. Her annual turnover crossed ₹18 lakh in just 3 years.
STEP Training Graduate

Anita Devi
Agriculture · Bhubaneswar, Odisha
"Farming was seen as a man's job. Now I lead a group of 20 women farmers and we supply to the city."
Anita grew up in a farming family but was never taken seriously when she expressed interest in managing the farm. After attending a women entrepreneurship workshop, she formed a collective of 20 women farmers in her village. They switched to organic farming, registered as a group on ShaktiUdyam, and started supplying organic vegetables directly to urban markets in Bhubaneswar. Their income has tripled compared to what the men in the village were earning through traditional farming methods.
Stand-Up India Beneficiary

Sunita Rao
Technology · Bengaluru, Karnataka
"The tech industry said it was not for women. I built a company with 15 employees and all of them are women."
Sunita worked as a software developer for 6 years before deciding to start her own company. She faced rejections from investors who doubted her ability to run a tech firm. She used the Mahila Udyam Nidhi scheme to get initial funding, completed a business leadership program, and launched TechHer Solutions — a web development and digital marketing agency. Today her company serves over 80 small businesses across South India and every single employee on her team is a woman.
Mahila Udyam Nidhi Beneficiary
